Second Generation Computer (1956-1963)

The second generation computers used transistors which were smaller and faster as well as consumed less power than vaccum tubes. ENIAC 1401; IBM 700 and CDC 160 are example of second generation computers.

Advantages
These computers were smaller in size and less bulky as compared to the first generation computers.
They generated lesser amount of heat than the first generation computes.
They were faster and less expensive as compared to the first generation computers.
Disadvantages
They were still costly and constant maintenance was required.
They required a cooling system.
They used punched cards for input.
They used assembly language which was also machine-dependent.
